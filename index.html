UNDERSTANDING HEADPHONE EQUALIZATION

A Guide to Better Sound

Sound from headphones is not always the same! Each headphone produces different frequencies (pitches) at different volumes (sound level). This change in volume over frequency is called the frequency response, and colors the sound signature of the headphone.

Tuning headphones is not straight forward! Natural preference for a headphone varies depending on ear shape, sensitivity to high pitched sounds, and love for bass.

Parametric Equalization Filters

How Equalization Works

Equalization allows you to adjust specific frequency ranges to match your preferred sound signature. By applying parametric filters, you can boost or cut particular frequencies to compensate for headphone characteristics.

Key Concepts

  • Frequency Response: How loud different pitches sound
  • Parametric EQ: Precise control over frequency, gain, and bandwidth
  • Target Curve: The ideal frequency response for neutral sound. This can also be customized to your preference
